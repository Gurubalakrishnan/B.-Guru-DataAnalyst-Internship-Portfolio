#### Statistical Validation Performed
A **Two-Sample T-Test** was conducted in Excel to compare **sales values between 2003 and 2004**.

**Hypothesis:**
- **Null Hypothesis (H₀):** There is no significant difference in average sales between 2003 and 2004.
- **Alternative Hypothesis (H₁):** There is a significant difference in average sales between 2003 and 2004.

**Observed values:**
- **Average Sales (2003):** 3516.98
- **Average Sales (2004):** 3512.389
- **Standard Deviation (2003):** 1763.679
- **Standard Deviation (2004):** 1775.285
- **P-value (T-Test):** 0.950436

**Interpretation:**  
Since the **p-value (0.950436)** is much greater than the significance level **0.05**, the null hypothesis is **not rejected**.  
This indicates that there is **no statistically significant difference** between the average sales of 2003 and 2004.

**Business Conclusion:**  
The sales performance between 2003 and 2004 remained relatively stable, and there is no evidence of a meaningful statistical change in average sales across these two years.
